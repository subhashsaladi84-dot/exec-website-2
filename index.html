Our mission

We fight for medical and mental health advocacy by meeting people where they are. Our work focuses on outreach, education, and direct navigation support so families can access care, understand options, and feel supported.

  • Mental health support and stigma reduction
    Workshops, peer support connections, and crisis resource education.
  • Access to care navigation
    Help finding clinics, financial assistance pathways, and appointment preparation.
  • Community partnerships
    We collaborate with other organizations to expand outreach and connect people to services faster.

Appointment Prep Checklist

Before the visit
  • Write down your main concern in one sentence.
  • List symptoms with start date, frequency, and what makes them better or worse.
  • Bring a list of medications and supplements with doses.
  • Bring allergies and past medical history, surgeries, and hospitalizations.
  • If you have records, bring discharge papers, test results, and imaging reports.
  • Confirm location, parking, time, and what documents you need.
Questions to ask
  • What do you think is most likely causing this?
  • What are other possibilities?
  • What tests do I need, and what will they tell us?
  • What should I do next if symptoms worsen?
  • What are the treatment options and side effects?
  • When should I follow up?
During the visit
  • Ask for plain language explanations if anything is confusing.
  • Ask for written instructions or a visit summary.
  • Confirm the next step before leaving: referral, test, medication, or follow up.

This checklist is educational and not medical advice. For emergencies call 911.

Patient Rights Quick Guide

  • Right to information: You can ask who is responsible for your care and request explanations you understand.
  • Right to respectful care: You deserve considerate, non discriminatory treatment.
  • Right to access records: Under HIPAA you generally have the right to access your medical records and billing records.
  • Right to ask questions: You can request risks, benefits, and alternatives in plain language.
  • Right to interpretation: If language is a barrier, request interpreting services.
  • Right to complain: If you believe privacy rights were violated, you can file a complaint with HHS OCR.

Lewisville to Dallas Fort Worth Community Clinic Directory

These are reliable starting points for finding low cost care, financial assistance, or a nearby health center. Availability changes, so always call first.

  • HRSA Find a Health Center: national locator for community health centers (sliding scale). Open locator
  • Parkland Financial Assistance: financial assistance for eligible Dallas County residents. Learn more
  • Parkland application portal: apply for Parkland assistance and charity discount. Apply here
  • Texas 211: find local clinics, mental health support, food, housing, and transportation resources. Open 211 Texas
  • 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline: immediate mental health crisis support. 988 website
